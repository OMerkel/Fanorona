Rules

Fanorona is played on a 9x5 point board. The game starts with 22 red and 22 yellow pieces and an empty center point.

Fanorona Goal

Win by leaving the opponent with no legal moves or no remaining pieces.

Fanorona Turn Sequence
  1. Red moves first.
  2. If at least one capture is available, a capture must be played.
  3. Captures happen by approach (moving toward opponents) or withdrawal (moving away).
  4. Multi-capture turns continue with the same piece; a repeated direction and revisiting points in the same turn are not allowed.
  5. If no capture exists, a single non-capturing Paika move is allowed to an adjacent connected point.
Vela Variant

Vela starts from the same initial position and has a phase-based opening.

  1. Phase 1: the designated capturer captures one piece per capturing move; the other side only plays non-capturing moves.
  2. Phase 1 ends after 17 captured pieces or when the non-capturing side reaches 5 pieces.
  3. Phase 2 continues with regular Fanorona rules.
